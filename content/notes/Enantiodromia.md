> [!quote]
> **Enantiodromia** ([Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language"): ἐναντίος, [romanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Ancient_Greek "Romanization of Ancient Greek"): _enantios_ – "opposite" and δρόμος, _dromos_ – "running course")
> 
> -- Source from  [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enantiodromia](Wikipedia)

A concept in Jungian psychoanalytics that postulates that the emergence of unconscious opposites in the course of time tries to restore the equilibrium to the psychological system. By trying to be hardworking, we create a sort of subconscious laziness.

In Discworld terms, it is the tendency of people or places to go so far into one direction that they emerge on the other side. Like people being so dumb that their inventions were in some way brilliant. A prime example is "Bloody Stupid" Johnson, who designed a mail sorting machine to have a central wheel with Pi (circumference to diameter ratio) of exactly 3, because he didn't like to calculate the rest of it. This caused the machine to tap into several dimensional layers, causing it to receive mail from future and alternative realms, making it extremely dangerous as well as adding a lot of extra work.
